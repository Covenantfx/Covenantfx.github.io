History of the Internet

    • 1957: U.S. forms the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) in response to the USSR launching Sputnik.
    • 1962: Licklider writes about a "galactic web" able to access information in electronic form.
    • 1965: First computer to computer connection.
    • 1969: ARPANET first established.
    • 1972: 23 hosts on the network, 50 KB/s. Ray Tomlinson created email.
    • 1973: TCP/IP is created.
    • 1974: TCP/IP is implemented.
    • 1981: The computer science network is founded.
    • 1981: ARPANET and the CSNET become cross-communicable.
    • 1983: DNS took hold and removed the use for ARPANET and CSNET, making TCP/IP.
    • 1985: First T-1 cable allowing speeds of 1.5 MB.
    • 1989: World Wide Web was created by Time Berners-Lee.
    • 1991: T-3 lines are implemented, allowing speeds of up to 45 MB/s.
    • 1991: CSNET became disused NREN rose (National research and education network).
    • 1995: Moore's law became realized (computer chips will double in power and half in price every 18 months).
    • 1995: YAHOO! became incorporated.
    • 1998: Satnet became implemented.
